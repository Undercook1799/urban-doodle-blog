Date: 27 August 2022

Social Media Surplus

Social media. Social media. Social media. We've all heard of it. Is it impossible to not use social media for most of us? Probably not - we take a break from Instagram, and few minutes later, we find ourselves on it again. Weird huh? This is a problem. We should ask ourselves some questions: how are these services running? Advertisements seem to be the biggest business model for now. Advertisements are shoved to our faces. Their goal is to compete for user's attentions so that they click or buy or share their product. What's linked to these advertisments is that every view and click gets linked to our digital profile. Think ID card - but on the internet. Internet ... nobody owns the internet. The internet doesn't die. Content is never erased on the internet. Our digital profiles makes us targets. Targets for advertisments, studies, personalization - all built off from our habits, actions, emotions, reactions. Yes, social media bring people together. Yes, social media lets us keep up to date with our friends & family. But this all comes at a cost. With newer generations getting accessed to social media at younger and younger ages, they are built up their digital profile to marketers.
